Maternal Mortality Reduction Strategy

A data-driven analysis of maternal mortality across all 37 Nigerian states, developed as a strategic intelligence report for UNICEF Nigeria's programme managers.

Using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn), I processed 2020 female mortality figures, built a correlation matrix heatmap, and produced scatter-plot visualisations that expose the direct relationship between antenatal care coverage and maternal death rates.

Strong inverse correlation (−0.96) between ANC coverage and maternal mortality — states with lower ANC attendance show dramatically higher death rates
Kebbi, Sokoto, and Zamfara identified as highest-risk states, each exceeding 800 deaths per 100,000 live births
Poor healthcare access shows a perfect positive correlation (1.00) with maternal mortality ratio — the single strongest predictor
Three-phase intervention roadmap proposed: immediate EmOC strengthening (0–12 months), scaling SBA deployment (12–36 months), and long-term systems integration (36+ months)

Afrilink — P2P Cross-Border Transfer App

A mobile-first fintech app enabling the African diaspora in North America to send money home — instantly, with zero transfer fees and live exchange rates. Designed to challenge LemFi and Chipper Cash by leading on UX simplicity and fee transparency.

Executed the full 7-stage UX design sprint: stakeholder research, competitive analysis (LemFi vs Chipper Cash vs Afrilink), user surveys, persona creation, task-flow mapping, lo-fi wireframing, hi-fi prototyping, and usability testing.

UI/UX Design · Podcast App · Social Impact 2024

Pod Noir — Black Women’s Podcast App

Designed a dedicated podcast platform for Black women — a community-first space to discover, listen to, and connect around podcasts made by and for Black women. Built using the 5-stage Design Thinking framework over 2 weeks, the project addressed a clear gap: the near-total absence of a dedicated audio platform centred on Black women’s stories, voices, and experiences.

Conducted primary and secondary research including user interviews, surveys, competitive analysis, empathy mapping, and usability testing. The research confirmed that 87.5% of users wanted community engagement features, and 83.3% preferred dark mode — both findings directly shaped the final design.

Research across 18–32 year olds revealed 78.2% listen to podcasts daily, yet struggle to find quality content by Black women buried beneath mainstream platform algorithms
Designed around 4 identified pain points: lack of diversity in mainstream platforms, limited discoverability, time-constraint-friendly episode formats, and safe handling of sensitive topics
Key persona “Maya Johnson” (marketing professional, busy commuter) shaped the Explore Topics grid, Follow flow, and Now Playing queue — prioritising speed and community over complexity
Dark mode UI informed by survey data (83.3% preference), amber accent palette chosen for warmth and cultural resonance, and community features (follow, 3.8k followers) built into the core navigation

Nature Nest — Plant Care App

Designed a comprehensive plant care mobile app aimed at helping plant owners of all skill levels — from busy urban apartment dwellers to seasoned botanical enthusiasts — diagnose plant problems, access care guides, connect with expert Plantvisors, and build a community around plant-keeping.

Led full UI/UX design using a 5-stage process: Understand, Define, Ideate, Design, and Takeaways. Research identified that a significant percentage of plant owners with limited horticultural knowledge struggle to diagnose plant problems compared to experienced gardeners — a gap Nature Nest bridges with AI identification, expert access, and educational content.

3 distinct user personas shaped the feature set: Busy Urbanite (reminders, quick-fix), Novice Gardener (plant ID, tutorials), and Plant Enthusiast (advanced diagnostics, community, smart device integration)
AI-powered plant identification feature lets users snap a photo to instantly identify species and receive targeted diagnosis — addressing the core pain point of self-diagnosing unknown plant issues
Plantvisor system connects users with verified plant disease experts for 1-on-1 chat and video consultations, reducing reliance on generic forum advice
Grower profile onboarding (Plant Enthusiast / Urban Gardener / Blossom Buddy / Botanical Explorer) personalises the entire app experience from the first session, tailoring care guides and content recommendations to skill level

UX Design · Stylist Booking · Live User Research 2024

StyleMate — Stylist Booking App

Designed a mobile app that connects users with professional fashion stylists for virtual one-on-one sessions — covering wardrobe consultation, trend exploration, and personalised outfit planning. The project ran a full UX process from audience definition through whiteboard user interviews, lo-fi sketching, and Figma hi-fi screens.

What makes this project distinctive is the live whiteboard research methodology: user interviews were conducted face-to-face with responses captured verbatim on a physical whiteboard, then photographed as primary research artefacts. The whiteboard journey map and wireframes were sketched live in the same session, grounding every design decision in real user words.

7 audience segments identified — from Fashion Enthusiasts and Busy Professionals to Gift Givers and Special Event Attendees — each with distinct needs, contexts, and entry points
Video chat emerged as the single most valued feature: "It let me show my wardrobe and get instant feedback" — driving the decision to gate unlimited video chat behind the Premium tier
6-screen whiteboard wireframe system (Home, Search, Schedule, Notifications, Profile, Chat) derived directly from the live user journey mapping session with persona "Sarah"
Premium membership model (Basic, Premium, VIP) designed around user-stated willingness to pay for multiple sessions — creating a sustainable revenue structure alongside the free tier

UI/UX Design · Social Reading App · Book Clubs 2024

BookLib — Social Book Club App

Designed a social reading mobile app where users create and join book clubs, track their reading progress interactively, vote in polls for the next club read, and stay connected with fellow members. The concept centres on discovery — users uncover new information about a book's creation, author background, and story inspiration as they make progress.

Unlike existing reading trackers that require manual data entry, BookLib supports the reading journey passively — tracking progress automatically and surfacing contextual information at the right moment. The design was validated through a competitive audit against Bookclubz, Bookship, and Novellic — BookLib outperformed all three across all five key features.

Competitive audit revealed BookLib is the only platform with all five key features: user-friendly UI, unlimited genres, reading progress tracker, club finder, and recommendation algorithm
User journey map tracked persona "Sam" through 6 emotional stages: Need → Opens App → Choose Books → Poll → Share Results → Start Reading — identifying a dip at app-open due to uncertainty about recommendations
Information architecture spans 4 core nav sections (Home, Search, Notifications, Account) with Trending Books, Currently Reading, and Book Club accessible from a single Home screen
5-stage design process: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Understand — using surveys, interviews, competitor analysis, information architecture, and usability studies
